Formal Research

I have not yet participated in any formal opportunities for research. Despite this, I do maintain an interest in select academic fields.

Regarding computer science, I am interested in utilizing the computational nature of algorithmic systems to produce more efficient algorithms for already existing processes and devising algorithmic approaches to relatively uninvestigated matters on an interdisciplinary scale. Cryptography also holds my interest as securing data in a responsible manner should be a primary objective for every existing data set. Data mining and reverse engineering methodologies also hold my interest.

Regarding economics, I am interested in the underlying factors which dictate how human beings will behave based on the limitations of their economic powers. With this sort of information available, I believe that we would be able devise an economically sound predictive model correlating biological/environmental attributes and decision-making processes. While I am aware that there exists a factor of unpredictability when it comes to predicting the actions of human beings, such a model as the aforementioned statement describes would be able to produce a recommended approximate direction for controlling the distribution of assets within the economy with respect to the targeted attributes.

Regarding philosophy, I am interested in the period of contemporary philosophy and different fundamental constructions regarding our perception of existence. The debate between existence preceding essence and essence preceding existence is a concern which I seek to answer as it can account for the justification of behaviors and stated purposes of progression among human beings. Ethics and pseudo-objectivism are also areas of interest in this regard. My primary goal is to add to the existing knowledge regarding unsolved problems in philosophy.

Regarding statistics, I am interested in improving upon existing statistical methods.

Informal Research

This category is dedicated to research which I have carried out for non-commercial or non-contractual purposes.

Random Number Generation in Warframe

Random number generation is the generation of a sequence of numbers or symbols that cannot be reasonably predicted better than by a random chance. In this paper I disclose the process of random number generation in the video game Warframe and clarify how it operates. Understanding random number generation is pivotal to the way individuals make decisions based on chance; it is my firm belief that those who demonstrate a consistency in logical reasoning will be more enticed to make informed decisions, and understanding the methodology to the system that is randomness and how it has been manipulated within the context of this video game is important to those who decide to participate in it. I have obtained the information presented in the paper through executable analysis, predictive analysis, and game testing with a dedicated group of theory testers.

Void Relics in Warframe

Upon the launch of Warframe Update: Specters of the Rail, a new way to acquire Prime items, Void Relics, was introduced. In this paper I disclose the rarity weights of the Void Relics. I have obtained the information presented in the paper through executable analysis.
